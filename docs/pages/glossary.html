GLOSSARY

Terms that define the tokenized real-world asset ecosystem

  • Real-World Asset (RWA): A tangible or intangible asset that exists off-chain, such as real estate, commodities, or bonds.
  • Tokenization: Converting ownership rights of a real-world asset into a digital token.
  • Security Token: A digital token representing ownership in a regulated asset.
  • Utility Token: Token granting access to a blockchain service or product.
  • Stablecoin: Cryptocurrency pegged to fiat or commodity value.
  • Fractional Ownership: Token-based shared ownership of an asset.
  • Smart Contract: Blockchain-based self-executing contract.
  • On-Chain: Data recorded directly on the blockchain.
  • Off-Chain: Data or actions occurring outside the blockchain.
  • Custodian: Secure holder of the physical asset backing a token.
  • Oracles: Data providers that link off-chain and on-chain info.
  • DeFi (Decentralized Finance): Financial systems built on public blockchains without intermediaries.
  • Liquidity: The ease of buying/selling an asset without slippage.
  • Yield Token: Token that represents income from an underlying asset.
  • RegTech: Regulatory technology aiding compliance.
  • KYC: Know Your Customer identity verification.
  • AML: Anti-Money Laundering protocols.
  • Proof-of-Reserve: Audit showing tokens are 1:1 backed.
  • Legal Wrapper: Legal structure binding on-chain tokens to off-chain assets.
  • Digital Twin: Digital mirror of a real-world asset.
  • Primary Market: Where tokens are first issued.
  • Secondary Market: Where tokens are traded post-issuance.
  • Programmable Asset: Tokenized asset with embedded smart logic.
  • Settlement Layer: Blockchain layer that finalizes ownership.
  • Composability: Ability of DeFi protocols to integrate with one another.
  • Token Generation Event (TGE): First issuance of tokens.
  • Security Token Offering (STO): Fundraising through compliant security tokens.
  • NFT (Non-Fungible Token): Unique digital token tied to a specific asset.
  • Token Standard: Technical framework for token behavior (e.g., ERC-20).
  • ERC-20: Standard for fungible tokens.
  • ERC-721: Standard for NFTs.
  • ERC-1155: Hybrid token standard supporting multiple token types.
  • Blockchain: Decentralized digital ledger of transactions.
  • Ledger: Transaction history database of a blockchain.
  • Public Key: Address used to receive tokens.
  • Private Key: Confidential key for authorizing transactions.
  • Cold Wallet: Offline token storage for high security.
  • Hot Wallet: Online token wallet for fast access.
  • Layer 1 Blockchain: Core base blockchain like Ethereum or Bitcoin.
  • Layer 2 Solution: Scaling enhancements layered on top of Layer 1.
  • Real Estate Tokenization: Converting property rights into tradable tokens.
  • Commodity Tokenization: Digitizing physical assets like oil or gold.
  • Treasury Tokenization: Tokenizing U.S. government bonds.
  • Collateralization: Using tokens/assets as loan security.
  • Asset-Backed Token: Token directly tied to a real-world asset.
  • Synthetic Asset: Token mimicking another asset’s price.
  • DAO: Decentralized Autonomous Organization governed by token holders.
  • Staking: Locking tokens to earn yield or support a network.
  • Burning: Removing tokens from circulation permanently.
  • Minting: Creating new tokens and putting them into circulation.
  • Gas Fees: Transaction fees paid on blockchain networks.
  • Whitepaper: Technical/financial explanation of a token/project.
