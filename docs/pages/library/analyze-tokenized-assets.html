How to Analyze Tokenized Assets

Analyzing tokenized assets involves examining both their blockchain-specific metrics and their real-world backing. Unlike traditional assets, tokenized versions introduce unique technical, legal, and market dynamics that must be assessed holistically.

1. Understand the Underlying Asset

Start by identifying what the token represents: gold, real estate, U.S. treasuries, carbon credits, or other assets. The risk profile of the real-world asset is your baseline.

2. Verify the Issuer

Look into who issued the token. Are they licensed? Are they compliant with local and international regulations? Read any published audits or attestations.

3. Examine Smart Contract Code

Make sure the token contract is verifiable and secure. If you’re not a developer, look for third-party audit reports or GitHub repositories for open-source code.

4. Check On-Chain Metrics

Review transaction volume, number of holders, contract interactions, and liquidity on decentralized exchanges. Compare it to the volume of the underlying traditional market.

5. Premium or Discount

Compare the token’s price to the actual market value of the real-world asset. Tokens often trade at a premium or discount depending on accessibility, liquidity, and trust in the issuer.

6. Redemption Options

Some tokenized assets allow redemption for the real-world item they represent. Evaluate the process: is it legally enforceable, transparent, and affordable?

7. Jurisdictional Considerations

Tokens backed by U.S.-based assets may differ legally from EU-backed assets due to differing regulatory frameworks. Consider geographic location when evaluating trust and compliance.
