The Double Pedigree Strategy

The Double Pedigree Strategy is a foundational methodology for comparing tokenized assets with their traditional counterparts. This approach enables investors to evaluate premium/discount levels, yield disparities, and performance benchmarks in a single, intuitive framework.

Why It Matters

Tokenized assets offer fractional ownership and liquidity, but are often misunderstood or mispriced when compared to their traditional equivalents. The Double Pedigree Strategy brings structure to this comparison by establishing:

  • Reference metrics (price, volume, yield)
  • Correlated time series
  • Visual divergence tracking
  • Risk factor overlay

Core Components

The strategy compares each tokenized asset to two distinct benchmarks:

  1. Primary Traditional Asset: The original security or commodity that backs the token (e.g., XAUT to Gold, Ondo USDY to Treasuries).
  2. Sector Peers: Other tokenized versions of the same asset class, to assess market equilibrium and anomalies.

Use Cases

The Double Pedigree model has become increasingly useful for:

  • Institutional arbitrage strategies
  • Stability analysis in emerging token markets
  • Real-time asset pricing transparency
  • Yield-to-risk visualization dashboards
