Smart Contracts Guide

Smart contracts are self-executing programs that run on a blockchain and automatically enforce the rules of an agreement. They are the backbone of tokenized assets and decentralized finance (DeFi).

What Is a Smart Contract?

A smart contract is code stored on a blockchain that executes predetermined actions when specific conditions are met. Once deployed, the code cannot be changed — ensuring transparency and immutability.

Why They Matter for Tokenization

  • They control how tokens behave (minting, burning, transfers).
  • They enable automation, like yield payouts or redemption requests.
  • They reduce the need for intermediaries (lawyers, banks, escrow agents).

Audit and Risk

Because smart contracts are immutable, bugs and vulnerabilities can be costly. Reputable token issuers will publish third-party audit reports. Always check the contract address on block explorers like Etherscan or Polygonscan.

Examples

  • ERC-20: The standard for fungible tokens like PAXG or USDC.
  • ERC-721: For non-fungible tokens (NFTs).
  • Custom Contracts: Built for complex logic such as token buybacks or interest-bearing wrappers.

Smart contracts are the programmable layer of tokenization. They replace traditional legal infrastructure with transparent code.
