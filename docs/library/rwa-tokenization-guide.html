RWA Tokenization Guide

Real-world asset (RWA) tokenization is the process of creating blockchain-based tokens that represent ownership of traditional assets like real estate, commodities, bonds, and more.

What Is It?

Tokenization bridges the traditional finance world with the digital economy. Instead of trading a gold bar or a paper bond, you own a token that is backed by that real-world item and recorded on a blockchain.

How Does It Work?

A trusted issuer holds custody of the real-world asset and creates a digital token that represents a claim on it. These tokens can then be bought, sold, and transferred like cryptocurrencies.

Benefits of Tokenization

  • Fractional Ownership: Enables small investors to buy a piece of large assets like real estate.
  • 24/7 Liquidity: Markets never close. Trade at any time.
  • Faster Settlement: Smart contracts can reduce clearing times from days to minutes.
  • Global Access: Anyone with an internet connection and wallet can participate.

Risks to Consider

  • Regulatory uncertainty
  • Issuer risk and custody concerns
  • Smart contract vulnerabilities
  • Lack of secondary market liquidity in some cases

Popular Use Cases

  • Gold-backed tokens (e.g. PAXG, XAUT)
  • Tokenized U.S. Treasuries
  • Tokenized real estate funds
  • Private credit on-chain
