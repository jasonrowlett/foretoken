### A
**Asset Tokenization** – The process of representing ownership of real-world assets (like real estate or commodities) on a blockchain as digital tokens.  
**Automated Market Maker (AMM)** – A smart contract-based system for swapping assets without traditional order books.

### B
**Blockchain** – A decentralized, cryptographically secure ledger for recording digital transactions.  
**Bond Token** – A digital token that represents a share of a bond or fixed income instrument.

### C
**CBDC (Central Bank Digital Currency)** – A digital currency issued by a central bank.  
**Collateralization** – Using one asset to back the value of another, often used in DeFi.

### D
**DAO (Decentralized Autonomous Organization)** – An on-chain governance model for community-led operations.  
**DeFi (Decentralized Finance)** – A system of financial products operating on public blockchains without intermediaries.

### E
**Ethereum** – A smart contract blockchain that enables tokenized applications and DeFi.

### F
**Fiat Currency** – Government-issued currency not backed by a physical commodity.

### G
**Governance Token** – A token that gives holders voting rights in a protocol or DAO.

### H
**Hard Asset** – A tangible, physical asset like gold, real estate, or equipment.

### I
**Interoperability** – The ability for blockchains and platforms to work together and share data.

### K
**KYC (Know Your Customer)** – A regulatory process for verifying user identity.

### L
**Liquidity** – How easily an asset can be bought or sold without affecting price.

### M
**Market Cap** – The total value of all tokens in circulation, calculated by price × supply.  
**MiCA** – Markets in Crypto Assets regulation from the EU governing digital assets.

### P
**Private Credit Token** – A token representing debt or fixed yield not available to public investors.

### R
**Real-World Asset (RWA)** – A physical or financial asset represented digitally on-chain.

### S
**Stablecoin** – A token pegged to a fiat currency, like USDC or USDT.

### T
**Tokenization** – Converting rights to an asset into a digital token on the blockchain.

### V
**Volatility** – The degree of variation in an asset’s price over time.
